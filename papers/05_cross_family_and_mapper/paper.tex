\begin{abstract}
We apply Mapper to the 24{,}885-protein frozen ESM-2 t30 embedding using a PCA-2 lens, a 10$\times$10 cover with 30\% overlap, and DBSCAN clustering ($\varepsilon{=}0.05$, min\_samples 5) per pre-image. The decomposition yields 149 nodes of which 6 are entirely positive, 30 are positive-enriched ($\text{pos\_frac} > 0.5$), and 88 are entirely negative. The top two positive nodes (PCA bins $(7,4)$ and $(7,3)$) contain approximately 3{,}000 proteins at 88--94\% positive rate, collectively representing $\approx 40\%$ of all positives in the dataset. Positives form a spatially contiguous cluster on the manifold rather than a diffuse distribution. Partitioning the distant-stratum failures by Pfam family (pending complete annotation; 425 of 24{,}885 covered as of April 12, 2026) is the natural next question: are the distant-stratum failures within-family (missed distant homologs of known toxin families) or cross-family (genuinely novel mechanisms)? This paper is released as v0.9 with the Mapper result complete; the Pfam partition is deferred to v1.0 when full annotation is in.
\end{abstract}

\section{Biological implication}
Spatial localization of the positive class suggests toxins share consistent PLM-encoded features across diverse sequence families. This is consistent with the hypothesis that ESM-2 encodes functional mechanism rather than mere sequence similarity. However, the homology cliff shows that this encoding fails on the distant stratum: test proteins far from any panel member in embedding space are systematically misclassified (Paper 1). A Mapper-augmented panel sampled from these positive-enriched nodes does \emph{not} rescue the cliff (see Paper 2, Section Mapper-biased panel augmentation). The positives-cluster-densely finding therefore serves as diagnostic of the cliff mechanism rather than a rescue lever.

\section{Cross-family partition (pending)}
Pending full Pfam annotation of the 24{,}885-protein test set, the distant-stratum false alarms (predicted positive, actually negative; 41 of 44 at t30 $R{=}1000$ $k{=}25$ per Paper 3) will be partitioned by whether their top-25 nearest panel neighbors share any Pfam identifier. If yes, the cliff failure mode is within-family (distant-homology confusion); if no, it is cross-family (genuinely novel mechanism misattribution). This partition is crucial for biosecurity deployment: within-family failures are correctable by panel expansion, cross-family failures are not. This analysis is registered for v1.0 once UniProt Pfam coverage reaches $\ge 90\%$ of the 24{,}885 accessions.
