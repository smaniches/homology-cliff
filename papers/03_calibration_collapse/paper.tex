\begin{abstract}
We compute reliability diagrams per similarity stratum for frozen ESM-2 t30 cosine $k{=}25$ retrieval on 24{,}885 biosecurity-relevant proteins with panel $R{=}1000$, seed 20260410. Expected Calibration Error increases 4.3-fold from close stratum (0.069) to distant stratum (0.294). Positive-prediction precision collapses from 0.788 to 0.068 across the same gradient: on the distant stratum, the classifier makes 44 positive predictions of which only 3 are true positives. The high-confidence failure mode is particularly severe: in the vote-fraction bin $[0.9, 1.0)$ on the distant stratum, the classifier makes 3 positive predictions, 0 of which are correct. For biosecurity deployment this implies a cosine k-NN pipeline at the regime described would generate a 93.2\% false-alarm rate on distant-stratum hits, operationally unusable without either a different metric (the learned-projection companion paper shows a 2.6$\times$ improvement) or human review of all distant-stratum flags.
\end{abstract}

\section{Precision}
At the decision threshold vote-fraction $\ge 0.5$ (majority positive): close precision $0.788$ (6152/7810), moderate $0.347$ (125/360), distant $\mathbf{0.068}$ (3/44). The number of distant-stratum positive predictions (44) is already small, and the vast majority (41 of 44) are false alarms.

\section{Implications for biosecurity}
Retrieval pipelines that assume vote-fraction reflects true positive probability across strata will systematically over-flag distant proteins as toxins. The calibration asymmetry is not correctable by a single global Platt or isotonic fit because the miscalibration is stratum-dependent. Either (i) the classifier is conditioned on stratum (per-stratum calibration), (ii) the classifier switches metrics (see companion learned-projection paper), or (iii) all distant-stratum positive hits go to human review. Operationally, option (iii) is safe; options (i) and (ii) reduce the human-review burden.
